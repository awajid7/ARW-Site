Ethics & Compliance

Effective Date: September 3, 2025

‎

At Arrow Consolidated, integrity, transparency, and accountability are non-negotiable. This Ethics & Compliance Statement sets out the standards that guide our decisions and behavior across all operations, relationships, and markets.

This Statement applies to all directors, officers, employees, contractors, and third-party partners acting on Arrow’s behalf.

‎

1. Integrity & Fair Dealing

We act honestly and fairly in all business activities. We deliver on commitments, avoid conflicts of interest, and make decisions that withstand public and client scrutiny.

2. Compliance with Laws & Regulations

Arrow complies with applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards in the locations where we operate. When rules differ, we follow the stricter standard where feasible.

3. Anti-Bribery & Anti-Corruption

  • Zero tolerance for bribery, kickbacks, facilitation payments, or improper advantages.
  • Gifts, hospitality, and expenses must be modest, lawful, and not intended to influence decisions.
  • Third parties must adhere to equivalent anti-corruption standards.
  • ‎

4. Conflicts of Interest

Personal interests must not compromise objective decision-making. Actual or perceived conflicts must be disclosed promptly to management for review and mitigation.

5. Confidentiality, Security & Data Protection

  • We protect confidential and proprietary information entrusted to us.
  • Personal data is handled lawfully and securely, in line with applicable privacy regulations.
  • We maintain appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards.
  • ‎

6. Data Ethics & Responsible Technology

  • • We use data and automation responsibly, aiming to minimize bias and respect human oversight.
  • • We are transparent about material limitations of tools and methodologies used.
  • • We avoid deploying technology in ways that could cause undue harm.
  • ‎

7. Human Rights, Labor & Workplace Conduct

  • • We support internationally recognized human rights and safe working conditions.
  • • Discrimination, harassment, retaliation, or exploitation is prohibited.
  • • We promote diversity, inclusion, and equal opportunity.
  • ‎

8. Environmental Responsibility

We seek to minimize environmental impact through responsible operations, efficient resource use, and consideration of sustainability in procurement and partnerships.

9. Fair Competition & Antitrust

We compete vigorously yet fairly. We do not engage in price-fixing, bid-rigging, market allocation, or misuse of competitively sensitive information.

10. Marketing, Communications & Records

  • • Public statements must be truthful, accurate, and not misleading.
  • • Claims are supported by evidence and appropriate approvals.
  • • Business records are kept complete, accurate, and retained per policy and law.
  • ‎

11. Third-Party Partners & Supply Chain

We work with partners and suppliers who align with our standards. We reserve the right to audit, request remediation, or terminate relationships where material breaches occur.

12. Speaking Up: Reporting Concerns

We encourage prompt reporting of suspected misconduct, policy violations, or illegal activity.

  • Email: compliance@arrowconsolidated.com
  • Reports can be made in good faith without fear of retaliation.
  • Concerns are investigated impartially and addressed appropriately.
  • ‎

13. Enforcement & Consequences

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contracts, and reporting to authorities where required.

14. Training & Awareness

Team members receive periodic training on ethics, compliance, data protection, and information security relevant to their roles.

15. Governance & Oversight

Management is responsible for maintaining effective compliance controls and culture. Material risks and incidents are escalated to senior leadership for review and remediation.

16. Updates to This Statement

We may revise this Statement periodically to reflect legal, regulatory, or operational changes. Updates are effective upon posting with a revised Effective Date.
